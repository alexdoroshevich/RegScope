## AI-generated content may be wrong

FedComment uses large language models (LLMs) and statistical machine learning
techniques to generate:

- Cluster labels and summaries.
- Retrieval-augmented (RAG) answers to user questions.
- Astroturf campaign likelihood scores.

**All AI-generated output may contain errors, omissions, hallucinations, or
misinterpretations.** It is produced by statistical models that do not
understand, verify, or attest to the truth of the content they summarise.

Do not rely on FedComment's output for any decision of legal, financial,
medical, regulatory, or personal consequence without independent verification
against primary sources.

## Data sources and privacy

FedComment ingests publicly available comment data from Regulations.gov and
related US federal APIs. This data may include submitter names, organisations,
and comment text that were submitted as public record.

To reduce re-publication and privacy risk, FedComment **anonymises submitter
names by default** in its user interface. Users may opt in to reveal original
names by explicitly toggling that option, in which case they acknowledge they
are viewing public-record data and accept responsibility for its use.

FedComment does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information
about users of the software itself. See [PRIVACY.md](PRIVACY.md).
